How to Get Your Image Link

G Google Drive
  • 1 Upload your image to Google Drive.
  • 2 Right-click the file and select Share.
  • 3 Under "General access", change it from Restricted to Anyone with the link.
  • 4 Click Copy link and paste it here.
  • 5 Any Drive URL format works — the portal normalizes it automatically.
https://drive.google.com/file/d/1aBcDeFgHiJkLmNoPqRsTuVwXyZ/view?usp=sharing
The portal converts your Drive link to a thumbnail format automatically. You don't need to edit the URL.
BB ImgBB (Free Image Hosting)
  • 1 Go to imgbb.com and upload your image — no account needed.
  • 2 After upload, you'll see several link options.
  • 3 Copy the Direct Link (not the viewer page URL).
  • 4 Paste it here — it works immediately.
https://i.ibb.co/xxxxxx/your-image-name.jpg
ImgBB direct links are the most reliable option. Recommended for announcements that need to stay live long-term.
CL Cloudinary
  • 1 Log into your Cloudinary account and go to Media Library.
  • 2 Click your image and select Copy URL.
  • 3 Make sure the URL ends in a file extension like .jpg, .png, or .webp.
  • 4 Paste it here — it passes through directly.
https://res.cloudinary.com/your-cloud-name/image/upload/v1234567890/sample.jpg
↗ Any Public Image on the Web
  • 1 Find any image on the web — Google Images, Wikipedia, a news site, anywhere.
  • 2 Right-click the image.
  • 3 Select Copy image address (Chrome) or Copy image link (Firefox/Safari).
  • 4 Paste it here — if the image is publicly accessible, it will load instantly.
https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Camponotus_flavomarginatus_ant.jpg/320px-ant.jpg
⚠️ Some websites block external image loading (hotlink protection). If the preview shows the placeholder instead of the image, use ImgBB or Google Drive to re-host it.
